Why Agile Ventures?

To survive, charities and non-profits need better, more effective and lower cost software solutions.

Better Software

First we to talk about your business, people and needs. Better software comes from our better tools and methods: Agile, rapid prototyping, Lean Startup. We work with you to make your fundraising and delivery more effective by better Websites, Mobile, Apps, Process Automation and projects.

Our purpose aligns with yours

Our purpose is to enable non-profit organisations to become more effective and efficient. As a non-profit ourselves, our aim is your aim – to overcome obstacles so you can help thousands more people in need.

Much lower cost

Our work is Open Source, so no licence fees, no supplier lock-in, instead open API’s and access to lots of free modules. Because we are non-profit, we pass on our lower costs: no overheads, lower staff costs and no VAT. If we build a shared platform for your solution, we can reduce your costs much further.

About

AgileVentures develops better software at lower cost to enable non-profits to be more effective and more efficient.

  • 2012

    Our Humble Beginnings

    In 2012 UC Berkeley's Software as a Service was the first of Massively Open Online Class (MOOC) focused on modern software engineering. Only the first half of the course was released, but there were tantalizing mentions of group projects.

  • 2013

    A Community is Born!

    All through 2013 the process and members evolved. The "LocalSupport" project was deployed, improved, and started to be noticed. The domain "agileprojects.org" was not available, but "agileventures.org" was, and so AgileVentures was born.

  • 2014

    Explosion of Projects

    More and more programmers get involved to contribute to good causes online, starting new and varied initiatives like the OSRA project.

  • July 2014

    New Horizons

    In collaboration with UC Berkeley, AgileVentures is creating paid project courses and delivering Agile Ventures premium services in collaboration with Swedish bootcamp Craft Academy.
